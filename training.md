# Training Plan

**Objective:** drop fat, hold muscle, build functional athleticism. Lean, not bulky.
**Cap:** 1 hour in the gym, 3–5 days/week. Bike and walking do the conditioning.

## Why this shape works for cutting

When you eat in a deficit, your body looks for a reason to keep muscle. Lifting reasonably heavy is that reason. So the lifts stay strength-focused (5–10 reps, real load), and the fat loss comes from food plus the bike and walking. Turning the weights into light high-rep "toning" circuits would burn a few more calories in the moment and cost you the muscle you want to keep. We don't do that.

## The rotation: A / B / C full-body

Three full-body sessions that rotate in order. You don't assign them to fixed weekdays. Whenever you get to the gym, you do the next letter. Miss a day, no problem, you just pick up where the rotation left off. The dashboard tracks which letter is next.

Each session: one main lower lift, one main press, one main pull, one accessory, one core/carry. Three working sets each. In and out inside an hour.

### Day A — Squat focus
1. Back Squat — 3 × 6–8
2. Flat DB Bench — 3 × 8–10
3. Pull-up / Lat Pulldown — 3 × 6–10
4. Romanian Deadlift — 3 × 8–10
5. Farmer Carry — 3 × ~40m

### Day B — Hinge focus
1. Trap Bar / Conventional Deadlift — 3 × 5–6
2. Standing Overhead Press — 3 × 6–8
3. Barbell / DB Row — 3 × 8–10
4. Walking Lunge — 3 × 10–12 per leg
5. Hanging Leg Raise — 3 × 8–12

### Day C — Single-leg + push/pull
1. Bulgarian Split Squat — 3 × 8–10 per leg
2. Incline DB Press — 3 × 8–10
3. Chin-up / Cable Row — 3 × 8–10
4. Hip Thrust — 3 × 10–12
5. Ab Wheel / Pallof Press — 3 × 8–12

Warm up 5 minutes: the 1.2-mile walk to the gym counts. Add 1–2 light ramp sets on the first lift.

## Progression rule (the dashboard does this automatically)

For each lift you log weight, reps, and RPE (how hard the set felt, 6 = easy, 10 = nothing left).

- Hit the **top** of the rep range on all sets at RPE 8 or lower → weight goes **up** next time (5 lb upper body, 10 lb lower).
- Land **inside** the range → repeat the same weight, aim for more reps.
- Miss the **bottom** of the range → weight comes **down** a notch, rebuild.

This is autoregulation. On a good day you push, on a flat day you hold, and you never grind yourself into the ground while dieting.

## Weekly template (example, 4 gym + 2 rides)

| Day | Session |
|-----|---------|
| Mon | Lift (next letter) |
| Tue | Zone-2 bike, 18–20 mi easy |
| Wed | Lift (next letter) |
| Thu | Rest / walk |
| Fri | Lift (next letter) |
| Sat | Tempo ride or long ride |
| Sun | Rest / family walk |

If you only get 3 gym days, run A/B/C across the week and lean on two rides. If you get 5, add a fourth lift and keep one ride.

## The bike (your fat-loss engine)

You're at 15–20 mi at 15.5 mph. Two jobs for it:

- **Zone 2 (easy, can hold a conversation):** the bread and butter. Burns fat, barely taxes recovery, so it doesn't fight your lifting. Build distance first. Target: 20 → 25 mi over the month, same easy effort.
- **Tempo / intervals (once a week):** 6 × 2 min hard / 2 min easy, or hold a faster pace on the flats. This nudges your engine up so 15.5 mph starts feeling easy.

The 2.4-mile round-trip walk to the gym is free movement. On lifting days you're already banking it. Don't skip it to drive.

## Readiness-guided load (Oura)

Each morning the dashboard reads your Oura readiness and gives one of three calls:

- **GO (80+):** recovered. Chase the top of the ranges, add load where it moves clean.
- **STEADY (65–79):** run it as written, no forced PRs.
- **EASY (under 65):** drop working weights ~15%, or trade the lift for a Zone-2 ride and mobility.

Dieting plus training plus four kids means recovery swings. This keeps you productive without digging a hole.

## Muscle-retention checklist

- Protein target hit most days (see nutrition.md).
- Weights trending up or holding, not collapsing.
- Sleep protected where you can (Oura will show you the cost of the late nights).
- Deficit aggressive but not reckless. Losing 4+ lb/week for weeks is where muscle starts going.
